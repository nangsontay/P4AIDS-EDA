💡 Key Insights

▼

Dataset Characteristics & Recommendations

  • ⚠️ Severely Imbalanced Target: 39,922 "no" vs 5,289 "yes" (Ratio = 7.55:1). Handling class imbalance is mandatory.
  • ✅ No Missing Values (NaN), No Duplicates — but "unknown" string values in contact (13,020), poutcome (36,959), education (1,857), job (288).
  • ⚠️ Heavy Skewness & Outliers: balance (mean 1362 vs median 448), duration (mean 258 vs median 180), pdays (median -1 meaning never contacted).
  • 🔑 poutcome = "success" is the single strongest predictor for subscription (~65% yes rate).
  • ⚠️ duration is highly predictive BUT known only after call ends — data leakage risk. Drop for pre-call predictive models.
  • ✅ Low correlations between numerical features (good for modeling). Notable: pdays-previous r=0.45.
  • 🔑 Students and retirees show higher subscription rates relative to group size (31% and 25% respectively).
  • 💡 Consider dropping duration for pre-call predictive models to avoid data leakage.
